Christmas is a widely celebrated annual festival commemorating the birth of Jesus Christ, observed on December 25 as a religious and cultural holiday by billions of people around the world. While Christmas has its roots in Christianity, it has become a secular holiday celebrated by people of various faiths and backgrounds.

Tradition

Christmas marks the birth of Jesus Christ, who is considered the Son of God by Christians. The story is detailed in the New Testament of the Bible, particularly in the Gospels of Matthew and Luke.

Date

While the exact date of Jesus' birth is not known, December 25 has been widely accepted as the day to celebrate Christmas. It was chosen to coincide with existing pagan festivals and as a way to Christianize these celebrations.

Christmas is associated with festive decorations, including Christmas trees, lights, ornaments, and wreaths. Exchanging gifts is a common Christmas tradition, symbolizing the gifts given to Jesus by the Three Wise Men.
